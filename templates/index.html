How It Works

Simple three-step process for plant identification and analysis

1. Upload Plant Image

Capture or upload a clear image of the medicinal plant you want to identify. Our AI works best with focused shots of leaves, flowers, or fruits.

2. AI Processing

Our Xception neural network analyzes the image with 96.79% accuracy, comparing against 200+ medicinal plant species in our database.

3. Get Detailed Analysis

Receive comprehensive data including medicinal properties, toxicity levels, active compounds, preparation methods, and conservation status.

Research Methodology

Scientific approach to medicinal plant analysis

1

Dataset Collection & Curation

Collected and curated dataset of 200+ medicinal plants with 12 pharmacological parameters each, including toxicity levels, medicinal value, and active compounds.

2

Model Architecture Design

Implemented Xception neural network architecture optimized for plant image classification with depthwise separable convolutions for efficiency.

3

Training & Validation

Achieved 93.34% training accuracy and 96.79% validation accuracy using transfer learning and data augmentation techniques.

4

Web Deployment

Developed Flask-based web interface with real-time processing capabilities and comprehensive result presentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Herbalist AI

What is the accuracy of Herbalist AI?
The system achieves 93.34% training accuracy and 96.79% validation accuracy using the Xception neural network architecture, making it highly reliable for medicinal plant identification.
How many plant species can it identify?
Herbalist AI can identify 200+ medicinal plant species, each with comprehensive pharmacological data including toxicity levels, medicinal value, active compounds, and preparation methods.
What information does it provide about identified plants?
For each identified plant, you receive 12 parameters: Scientific name, biological name, medicinal value, family, common uses, active compounds, toxicity level, geographical distribution, growth habit, parts used, preparation methods, and conservation status.
Is this a commercial or research project?
This is an academic research project conducted by a Computer Science student from IUST Kashmir. It focuses on applying deep learning techniques to medicinal plant analysis and traditional medicine preservation.
What technology stack is used in this project?
The project uses TensorFlow for deep learning, Xception CNN architecture, Python Flask for backend, OpenCV for image processing, Pandas for data handling, and modern web technologies for the frontend interface.
